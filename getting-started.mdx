## Welcome to Punchey

This short guide helps you get set up quickly, even if you are not tech savvy. You will sign in, explore the dashboard, connect payments, add a few customers or services, and try a test payment.

## New account checklist

Your first day is about getting the essentials in place so your account works the way you need. This section mirrors the high-level structure of the Freshdesk onboarding materials and keeps things simple.

- Confirm your login and verify your business details.

- Set your business logo and basic profile settings.

- Add or edit your registers so payment activity is routed correctly.

- Configure cash management controls so drawers, tills, and users follow the right rules.

- Link or update the bank account used for payouts.

- Create user accounts for your team and assign permissions.

- Set up your Smart Number for customer calls and texts.

- Import your existing contacts to make communication easier from day one.

For detailed step-by-step instructions, screenshots, and troubleshooting, use the Freshdesk Get Started resources:\
[https://puncheyteam.freshdesk.com/en/support/solutions/158000396728](https://puncheyteam.freshdesk.com/en/support/solutions/158000396728)

## Understand your account header

A quick look at the top of your screen helps you get oriented. The header gives you access to:

- Your location, register, and active session.

- Notifications and quick actions.

- Your profile menu where you manage account settings and billing.

If you want deeper guidance on each menu or icon, the Help Center provides a full visual walkthrough.

## What to do first

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to your account" icon="arrow-right" title-type="p">
    - Open your welcome email and follow the sign-in link.

    - Save your password in a secure place so you can return anytime.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Look around the dashboard" icon="monitor" title-type="p">
    - Explore the main menu on the left.

    - You will see tools for payments, customers, appointments, reviews, and settings.

    - Click through a few sections to understand what is available.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your payment method" icon="credit-card" title-type="p">
    - Go to your payout and payment settings.

    - Follow the prompts to connect your bank account.

    - This enables you to accept real customer payments.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a few customers and services" icon="users" title-type="p">
    - Create a test customer so you can practice transactions.

    - Add the core services or products you sell.

    - Keep it simple; you can adjust or expand later.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run a small test payment" icon="check-circle" title-type="p">
    - Use a test customer and a small amount.

    - This verifies everything is working and helps you get comfortable.

    - You can void or refund your test after you are done.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Core setup tasks

Once you have completed the basics, these items help prepare your account for day‑to‑day use:

- Add or edit registers so each location and terminal has the correct configuration.

- Set your business logo so receipts, invoices, and messages look professional.

- Configure cash management rules such as opening counts, closing counts, and cash accountability policies.

- Link or update your bank account for payouts and deposits.

- Set up team user accounts and define permissions in your location and team settings.

- Activate your Smart Number so clients can call or text you through Punchey.

- Import your customers or contacts to begin messaging, booking, and tracking activity immediately.

You can find full guides for each of these tasks across relevant documentation sections such as [Payment Solutions](/payment-solutions), [CRM Tools](/crm-tools), [Communication Tools & Smart Number](/communication-tools-and-smart-number), and [Team & Locations](/team-and-locations).
